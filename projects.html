Projects

Web Search Companion

During my senior year at Southern Oregon University (SOU) I teamed up with another student to develop the Web Search Companion (WSC), our Capstone Project. The client requested an internet search application with the following features:

  • the ability to locate online topics,
  • save and store the topic results for future use and
  • return to the results later for user updates

WSC was designed as a substitute to an existing Google search feature that the client didn’t feel was reliable enough for him. Also, the team decided to focus on functional development as opposed to the user interface (UI), so the UI was purposefully left undecorated.

Development was done in sprints and then presented to the product owner for suggested improvements. See a description of the entire project in the write up here.

Geographic Information Systems Data

For six months, I worked on a team at Dymaptic that developed features for a tablet application used by field workers of an oil and gas construction company who were unable to access wi-fi connections. The app recorded GIS survey points to inspect underground pipelines and document their condition for integrity management plans demonstrating compliance to safety regulations. Once recorded by the app, the information was then uploaded to an on-premise DynamoDB database. Survey data included images, electric current readings and field notes.

As a member of the coding team, I enjoyed collaborating with my teammates to create useful products that helped people get their jobs done. I received new feature assignments in sprint rounds, developed code, and did unit testing. I also worked on an in-house project that used the ESRI software ArcGIS Online and open source resources on GitHub.

I would like to do more with GIS data.
